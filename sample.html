Background & Purpose
Flooding in Panabo City

Flooding is one of the most common and destructive environmental hazards affecting communities worldwide. It occurs when heavy rainfall, poor drainage systems, rising river levels, or coastal conditions cause water to overflow into residential and public areas (Fowler & Kilsby, 2003).

In Panabo City, flooding is a recurring problem, especially in low-lying barangays. The city's vulnerability has increased due to changing weather patterns, intense rainfall, rapid urbanization, and improper waste disposal practices that clog drainage systems and waterways.

This portal serves as a centralized information hub delivering reliable data to citizens, LGU officers, and the DRRM Council — facilitating knowledge capture, sharing, storage, and application to ensure knowledge is used effectively, especially during disasters.

Reference: Fowler, H. J., & Kilsby, C. G. (2003). Implications of changes in seasonal and annual extreme rainfall. Geophysical Research Letters, 30(13), 1726. https://doi.org/10.1029/2003GL017327

🎯 Purpose

Create an accessible information hub for citizens, LGUs, and the DRRM Council with concrete flood preparedness plans and strategies.

🌱 Ground Subsidence

Panabo City has the highest subsidence rate in the Philippines at 38 mm/year due to groundwater extraction, fishpond operations, and industrial activities.

🌀 Climate Factors

The city is affected by typhoons, low pressure areas (LPAs), and La Niña, which brings above-normal rainfall. 2–8 typhoons are expected annually.

🏚️ Human Factors

Clogged drainage systems, improper waste disposal, and changes in land use contribute to faster water accumulation during rainfall events.

🚶 Evacuation Procedures

1
Receive official evacuation order from barangay captain or DRRMO officer via text, announcements, or sirens.
2
Grab your emergency kit: water, food, medicine, documents, flashlight, and extra clothing.
3
Turn off electricity, gas, and water at the main switches before leaving your home.
4
Proceed to your nearest designated evacuation center using elevated routes. Avoid flooded roads.
5
Register at the evacuation center and receive your family ID from the barangay health worker.
6
Wait for the official "All Clear" signal from DRRMO before returning home.
🚫 DO NOT return home until officially cleared. Do not walk through flooded roads. Do not use electrical equipment near water.
Disaster Preparedness
Preparedness Guide
  • 🗺️
    Know your risk: Check the flood hazard map above and identify if your area is prone to flooding. Know your barangay's risk level.
  • 🎒
    Prepare an emergency kit: Include food, water (at least 3 days supply), medicine, flashlight, batteries, important documents, and first aid supplies.
  • 🏠
    Secure your home: Elevate electrical appliances and important furniture, repair roof leaks, and clear your drainage channels and canals.
  • 📡
    Stay informed: Monitor weather updates from PAGASA, follow your barangay's official social media pages, and sign up for SMS alerts.
  • 📞
    Know your evacuation center: Identify the nearest center (see above) and plan your route using elevated roads away from flood-prone areas.
  • 🚨
    Evacuate immediately when advised by authorities or when water levels rise rapidly. Do not wait for the situation to worsen.
  • 🚫
    Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters: They may be deeper than they look and can carry debris, waste, and dangerous contaminants.
  • ⚡
    Stay away from power lines and electrical equipment: Do not touch wet switches, outlets, or appliances during flooding.
  • ⛰️
    Move to higher ground and follow all instructions from rescue teams and DRRM officers without delay.
  • 📱
    Communicate your location to relatives and barangay officials. Use SMS or social media to update your status and request help if needed.
  • ✅
    Wait for the official "all clear" signal from DRRMO or barangay officials before returning home.
  • 🔍
    Check for damage: Carefully inspect your house for structural issues, gas leaks, or electrical problems before entering.
  • 🧹
    Clean and disinfect: Remove mud and debris, and thoroughly sanitize surfaces, water containers, and food areas to prevent the spread of disease.
  • 📋
    Report damage and incidents: Inform the local DRRMO or barangay office about damage, injuries, and community needs using the form below.
  • 💊
    Monitor your health: Watch for symptoms of waterborne diseases (diarrhea, skin infections, leptospirosis). Seek medical help immediately if needed.
  • ♻️
    Practice proper waste management: Do not throw garbage in canals, rivers, or drainage systems — this is a major cause of flooding in Panabo City.
  • 🤝
    Participate in cleanup drives: Join your barangay's canal and drainage clearing activities, especially before the rainy season.
  • 📚
    Attend disaster preparedness seminars: Learn life-saving skills at CDRRMO-organized events and share that knowledge with neighbors and family.
  • 🌱
    Support sustainable practices: Reduce water usage and avoid activities that contribute to land subsidence, such as excessive groundwater extraction.
  • 📣
    Spread awareness: Share flood safety information with your community, especially with elderly residents and persons with disabilities.

ICT in Public Administration
How This System
Improves Governance

This ICT-based portal directly supports Panabo City's LGU by improving the four key areas of public administration and disaster management.

⚡

Disaster Response Time

Real-time incident reporting via the online form allows DRRMO to receive and act on reports faster, reducing the time between incident occurrence and official response.

🤝

LGU–Citizen Coordination

The portal bridges the gap between government agencies and citizens — enabling two-way communication through incident reporting, advisories, and public dashboards.

🔍

Transparency & Accountability

The public "Track Your Report" dashboard shows citizens the real-time status of their reports, holding LGU officers accountable and building public trust in governance.

🚀

Efficiency of Public Service

Centralized digital storage of DRRM plans, SOPs, and resources eliminates paperwork bottlenecks and ensures the right information reaches the right people instantly.
