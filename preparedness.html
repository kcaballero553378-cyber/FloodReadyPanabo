Disaster Preparedness
What To Do
  • 🗺️
    Know your risk: Check the flood hazard map and identify if your area is prone to flooding.
  • 🎒
    Prepare an emergency kit: Include food, water, medicine, flashlight, batteries, important documents, and first aid supplies.
  • 🏠
    Secure your home: Elevate electrical appliances and furniture, repair leaks, and clear drainage channels.
  • 📡
    Stay informed: Monitor weather updates from PAGASA and local announcements regularly.
  • 🚨
    Evacuate immediately if advised by authorities or if water levels rise rapidly.
  • 🚫
    Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters: They may be deeper than they look and can carry debris or contaminants.
  • ⚡
    Stay away from power lines and electrical equipment. Do not touch wet switches or outlets.
  • ⛰️
    Move to higher ground and follow all instructions from rescue teams and DRRM officers.
  • ✅
    Wait for the "all clear" signal before returning home.
  • 🔍
    Check for damage: Inspect your house for structural issues, gas leaks, or electrical problems.
  • 🧹
    Clean and disinfect: Remove mud and debris, and sanitize surfaces to prevent the spread of disease.
  • 📋
    Report incidents: Inform the local DRRMO or barangay office about damages and community needs.
  • ♻️
    Practice proper waste management: Do not throw garbage in canals, rivers, or drainage systems.
  • 🤝
    Participate in clean-up drives: Help maintain clear waterways in your barangay.
  • 📚
    Attend disaster preparedness seminars: Learn life-saving skills and share knowledge with others.
  • 🌱
    Support sustainable practices: Reduce water usage and avoid activities that contribute to land subsidence.
