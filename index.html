Background
The Flooding Problem
in Panabo City

Flooding is one of the most common and destructive environmental hazards affecting communities worldwide. It occurs when heavy rainfall, poor drainage systems, rising river levels, or coastal conditions cause water to overflow into residential and public areas (Fowler & Kilsby, 2003). This natural hazard can lead to widespread disruption, affecting transportation, public safety, health, and local economies.


In Panabo City, flooding is a recurring problem, especially in low-lying barangays. The city’s vulnerability to flooding has increased due to changing weather patterns, intense rainfall, rapid urbanization, and improper waste disposal practices that clog drainage systems and waterways. As a result, flooding has caused significant damage and losses to properties, livelihoods, and infrastructure.


🎯 Purpose

Create an accessible information hub for citizens, LGUs, and the DRRM Council with concrete flood preparedness plans and strategies.

🌱 Ground Subsidence

Panabo City has the highest subsidence rate in the Philippines at 38 mm/year due to groundwater extraction, fishpond operations, and industrial activities.

🌀 Climate Factors

The city is affected by typhoons, low pressure areas (LPAs), and La Niña, which brings above-normal rainfall. 2–8 typhoons are expected annually.

🏚️ Human Factors

Clogged drainage systems, improper waste disposal, and changes in land use contribute to faster water accumulation during rainfall events.
